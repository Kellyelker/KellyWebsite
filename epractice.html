Ethical Pracitice

During our ethics course, I research and studied a variety of ethical cases, particularly pertaining to adolescents as working in a school you are often working with minors. My final paper incorporated resolving a case scenario by taking the recommended steps by the Canadian Code of Ethics for Psychologists (picture included below, taken from EDPS 604, PowerPoint). This paper discusses the ethical principles and standards of practice regarding consent and confidentiality in a school setting.

“School counsellors make ethical decisions in an environment where there is often conflict between the age of majority, parents requesting information from counsellors, and each school board has rules and regulations that counsellors must follow (Lehr, Lehr, and Sumarah, 2007). For example, a parent’s ‘right to know’ must be balanced with the benefits and risks involved to invade the child’s privacy (Lehr, Lehr, and Sumarah, 2007). Counsellors can follow the CCA Code of Ethics (2017) and CCPA Standards of Practice (2008) to help guide them in decision making and develop policies on informed consent and confidential through dialogue on what they believe are the rights of students entering counselling services in the school environment (Lehr, Lehr, and Sumarah, 2007).” -Taken from my Integrative Essay, EDPS 604
