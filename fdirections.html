Future Directions

Graduating from the Masters of Counselling Program I can see myself moving towards a few different directions. Since my experience has always been geared towards working in education, I always assumed I would stay in education and become a school counsellor. Running two different mental health programs within Calgary Catholic have afforded me to the opportunity to interact with a wide variety of student populations. I love working with adolescents in the classroom, developing relationships with them, and coaching a variety of sports as well. I can see myself working in a school at least for another few years as I continue to develop my skills.

After my counselling practicum at Insight Counselling and Therapy centre, working in private practice and at a community centre has opened my eyes to counselling and working with adults. I enjoyed working with clients of all ages, ethnicities, religions, and backgrounds. I was able to employ a variety of therapeutic interventions as my supervisor was open with me experimenting with different techniques and methods, such as CBT, ACT, TA, and SFT.

My goal right now is to continue to enroll in professional development courses to gain experience in learning more about specific counselling interventions, such as DBT and couples counselling. In the distant future, I would like to write the EPPP and become provincial psychologist work as I work towards becoming a registered psychologist.
